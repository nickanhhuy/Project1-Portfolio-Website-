Ho Chi Minh City

Ho Chi Minh City, commonly known as Saigon, is the most populous city in Vietnam with a population over 10 million residents. This current name was given after the Fall of Saigon in 1975, and honors Ho Chi Minh, the former president laid the foundation for independence and sustainable development for Vietnam.
